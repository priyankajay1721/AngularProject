Arthritis: Causes, Symptoms, and Relief Strategies

Arthritis

Arthritis is a joint disorder featuring inflammation. A joint is an area of the body where two different bones meet. A joint functions to move the body parts connected by its bones. Arthritis literally means inflammation of one or more joints. Arthritis is frequently accompanied byjoint pain. Joint pain is referred to asarthralgia. There are many types of arthritis (over 100 identified, and the number is growing).

Symptoms

Arthritis can vary widely in their symptoms and severity, depending on the underlying cause. However, some common symptoms of Arthritis include:

1. Your joints may feel stiff and hard to move.

2.The area around your joints may look red or swollen or feel warm to the touch.

3.Your joints may feel painful to move or sore when you touch them..

4.Stiffness when you wake up or after youâ€™ve been sitting for a while

5.Tenderness -- the area is sore when you touch it

6.Grating -- you might feel things rubbing together inside the joint

7.An inability to use your full range of motion

8.Bone spurs, which feel like hard lumps around the joint

9.Swelling or changes to the shape of the joint

Relief Strategies

There are various strategies you can try to relieve Arthritis, depending on the type and severity of your Arthritis. Here are some common approaches:

1.Physical activity is a great way to manage and reduce arthritis pain. It can also help improve joint function.

2.Both heat and ice can manage your arthritis symptoms. Heat can increase blood flow to promote joint, muscle, and tissue healing and ease aches and stiffness.5 Malanga GA, Yan N, Stark J. Mechanisms and efficacy of heat and cold therapies for musculoskeletal injury. Postgrad Med. 2015;127(1):57-65. doi:10.1080/00325481.2015.992719 In contrast, cold can help ease pain and swelling.

3.According to the American College of Rheumatology and the Arthritis Foundation, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) are one of the most effective OTC remedies for managing OA pain.7 They can also help manage inflammatory arthritis by reducing pain and inflammation.

4.Acupuncture: This ancient Chinese medical practice uses thin needles inserted at specific points in the body. Research shows it offers short-term relief for people with arthritis.8 National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health. Arthritis and complementary health approaches: what the science says. While there is little evidence of its long-term benefits, the risk of harm with acupuncture is low.

5.The research on massage shows some evidence that massage is superior to nonactive therapies for reducing pain and improving function.8 Your options for massage include going to a day spa, getting a massage as part of physical therapy, and using handheld devices or massage chairs.

6.Research on mindfulness meditation, biofeedback, and relaxation training shows these therapies can improve function and offer higher pain tolerance in people with arthritis.10 These therapies are designed to bring about relaxation or mindful responses, resulting in a reduced negative response, slower breathing, reduced heart rate, and lower blood pressure, which is the opposite of a stress response.
